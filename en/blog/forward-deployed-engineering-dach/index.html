Why Forward Deployed Engineering Is the Future of Consulting

The FDE model explained: comparison with traditional consulting, its US origins at Palantir and OpenAI — and why the DACH region is two years behind the trend.

TL;DR

  • A Forward Deployed Engineer writes code in your codebase, instead of delivering slides.
  • The model originates in the US AI market (Palantir, OpenAI, Anthropic) and is standard there.
  • In the DACH region it’s still largely unknown — which makes it an opportunity for those who adopt it first.

The Problem with Traditional AI Consulting

Most AI projects in mid-market companies don’t fail because of the technology. They fail because of the distance between the people writing the strategy and the people writing the code. A traditional consulting firm delivers a strategy document — and is gone before the first line of code is written.

Forward Deployed Engineering turns that around. Instead of advising from the outside, an experienced engineer becomes part of your team: same chat, same repo, same backlog.

Where the Model Comes From

Palantir coined the term. Today it’s standard at every AI-first company — OpenAI, Anthropic, Scale AI, and Lovable deploy FDEs with their most important customers. The reason: AI products are context-dependent. Without proximity to the real use case, no workable solution emerges.

What Sets an FDE Apart from Alternatives

  • vs. consultant: writes code, not slides.
  • vs. body-leasing freelancer: takes ownership of architecture and strategy decisions.
  • vs. full-time hire: immediately productive, without 9 months of recruiting and notice periods.

Why the DACH Region Is Behind

The DACH consulting market is optimized for day rates and deliverables, not outcomes. The FDE model doesn’t fit the familiar pattern — which means the first companies to use it have a real speed advantage.

What You Can Do Now

  1. Check whether you have a concrete AI feature that needs to go live in the next few weeks.
  2. Ask yourself honestly whether your team can close the production gap alone.
  3. If not: let’s spend 30 minutes talking about the FDE model.
