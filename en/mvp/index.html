How I build MVPs

Speed doesn't come from magic — it comes from a deliberate tech stack. If you have a different backend (Django, Rails, .NET), I'll adapt — but the speed comes from ruthless simplification.

Frontend React + TypeScript, often via Next.js or Vite. Tailwind. shadcn/ui.
Backend Supabase or Node + Postgres. Lovable for UI-first iterations.
AI Stack OpenAI / Anthropic / Azure OpenAI, LangChain or direct SDK integration, pgvector for RAG.
Deployment Vercel or Cloudflare. Domain via you or via me.
Analytics PostHog (open source) or Plausible. Eval logs in Notion or Langfuse.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if the MVP is done and I want to keep developing?

Three options: (1) your team takes over — code handover is part of the package. (2) I stay on as FDE on retainer. (3) I hand off to another team with a detailed walk-through.

I don't have a tech team yet. Does this still work?

Yes — but we should discuss the handover aspect honestly. Without a tech team, you're either in an ongoing retainer with me after the MVP, or you'll need an internal setup afterward.

Can we share the risk — revenue share instead of fixed price?

In exceptional cases, yes. Prerequisites: concrete market model, transparent metrics, confidential agreement. For 80% of cases, fixed price is the fairer model for both parties.

Which industries won't you work in?

Gambling, adult content, weapons, politically toxic domains. Otherwise: largely open, from B2B SaaS to industrial to public sector.
